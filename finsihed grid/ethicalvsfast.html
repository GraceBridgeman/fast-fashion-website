Telling the difference

H&M, Zara, Rip Curl, Shien, Urban Outfitters, Gap, farmers, pretty little thing. These are all well known and popular for their cheap and trendy clothes. However these clothes are all contributing to fast fashion. Sometimes it can be difficult to tell the difference between fast fashion and ethical fashion. One way to tell the difference is to look at how much waste each one produces. If your clothes are often falling apart and breaking causing you to dispose of them they are obviously producing waste. If this is happening the brand is obivously bad quality and you should stop buying from them. Another thing to look at is how much the brand pays their workers and the conditions these workers work in.

Every year a company called tearfund releases an ethical fashion report. In the report they grade brands based on how ethical they are. The brands mentioned are all graded with an F. This tells us that they do not meet different ethical fashion standards. I recommend having a look at the tearfund ethical fashion report to become more informed about the behind the scenes of the brands you buy from. Understanding the differences between ethical fashion and fast fashion will help to make a difference.
